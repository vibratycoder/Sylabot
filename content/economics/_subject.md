# Economics

The study of how individuals, businesses, and governments allocate scarce resources. Bryant University's economics program covers both microeconomic and macroeconomic theory with emphasis on applied, real-world analysis. The department is housed within the College of Arts and Sciences, Department of Mathematics and Economics.

## Programs

- **Business Economics Major**: 30+ credits (6 required + 4 electives)
- **Economics Concentration**: 18 credits (ECO 313, 314, 315 + 3 electives)
- **Economics Minor**: 12 credits (ECO 313, 314, 1 additional, 1 at 400-level)

## Course Areas

- **Microeconomics** (ECO 113, 313, 413) -- Individual markets, firm behavior, consumer choice
- **Macroeconomics** (ECO 114, 314, 414) -- National economy, GDP, inflation, monetary/fiscal policy
- **Econometrics** (ECO 315, 415) -- Statistical methods for economic data
- **Research Methods** (ECO 210) -- Mathematical, statistical, programming skills for research
- **Mathematical Economics** (ECO 310) -- Mathematical methods for economic theory
- **Money and Banking** (ECO 201) -- Financial system, Federal Reserve, monetary policy
- **International Economics** (ECO 471) -- Trade theory, exchange rates, globalization
- **Applied Fields** -- Sports (340), Environmental (461), Labor (463), Health (473), Behavioral (464), Public Finance (462), Development (367), Industrial Organization (363/364)
