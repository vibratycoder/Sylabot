# Microeconomics

## ECO 113: Microeconomic Principles (3 credits)

Introduces fundamental microeconomic principles, covering the nature and method of economics, the roles of the private and government sectors, firm behavior, market structures, and resource distribution. No prerequisites.

## ECO 313: Intermediate Microeconomics (3 credits)

Analyzes firm behavior through demand, supply, and equilibrium analysis under perfect and imperfect competition, plus production factor allocation. Prerequisite: ECO 113.

## ECO 413: Applied Microeconomics: Case Studies (3 credits)

Examines microeconomic theory application to real business environments through case studies, analyzing supply, demand, market structures, government intervention, and resource markets. Prerequisites: ECO 113, ECO 114, junior standing.

## Topic Sequence

1. Introduction to Economics and Scarcity
2. Supply and Demand
3. Elasticity
4. Consumer Theory (Utility and Choice)
5. Production and Costs
6. Perfect Competition
7. Monopoly
8. Monopolistic Competition and Oligopoly
9. Factor Markets and Labor
10. Market Failures, Externalities, and Public Goods
11. Government Intervention and Regulation
