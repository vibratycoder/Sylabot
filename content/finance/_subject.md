# Finance

The study of money management, investments, and financial decision-making. Bryant's finance program prepares students for careers in corporate finance, investment banking, financial planning, and portfolio management.

## Bryant University Course Catalog (2025-2026)

### Core / Introductory
- **FIN 201** Financial Management -- Time value of money, risk/return, valuation, capital budgeting, cost of capital, capital structure
- **FIN 201G** Global Dimensions of Financial Management -- Same as FIN 201 with international emphasis

### Intermediate / Advanced
- **FIN 310** Intermediate Corporate Finance -- Capital budgeting depth, capital structure, dividend policy, working capital, valuation techniques, Excel modeling
- **FIN 312** Investments -- Risk/return measurement, security pricing, portfolio management, CAPM, market operations
- **FIN 315** Financial Institutions and Markets -- Banks, insurance companies, capital markets, financial instruments, regulatory framework
- **FIN 368** Multinational Finance -- International monetary system, hedging, arbitrage, foreign investment analysis, multinational working capital
- **FIN 370** Financial Statement Analysis -- Interpreting financial reports, firm valuation, management strategy in reporting
- **FIN 371** Archway Financial Statement and Security Analysis -- Real-firm equity analysis, sector teams, presentations to professionals
- **FIN 380** Financial Modeling -- Excel-based: loan amortization, lease analysis, capital budgeting, options pricing, CAPM, portfolio management
- **FIN 381** Risk Management and Insurance -- Non-speculative risk, risk identification/measurement, self-insurance, purchased insurance
- **FIN 383** Real Estate Finance -- Real estate instruments, methods, institutions, risk/return evaluation, mortgage markets
- **FIN 384** Credit Essentials -- Cash flow analysis, structuring commercial loans, business tax return analysis, industry analysis

### Capstone / Specialized
- **FIN 412** Introduction to Fintech and Digital Innovation -- Technology disruption in finance, fintech portfolio management
- **FIN 454** Archway Equity Portfolio Management -- Student-managed fund, asset allocation, diversification, performance measurement
- **FIN 458** Debt Securities, Derivatives and Investing -- Fixed income analysis, options, futures, pricing, risk management
- **FIN 460** Corporate Finance: Theory and Practice -- Capital budgeting, capital structure, leasing, M&A, LBOs, real options, hedging
- **FIN 465** Innovations in Contemporary Finance -- Financial engineering, advanced quantitative methods
- **FIN 466** Data Analysis for Finance -- Big data in finance, open source analytical tools, financial decision-making
- **FIN 475** Management of Banking Institutions -- Asset/liability management, depository institution profitability
- **FIN 480** Archway Fixed Income Portfolio Management -- Managing fixed income allocation within the Archway Investment Fund

### Financial Services (FS prefix)
- **FS 486** Securities Brokerage -- Financial planning, securities markets, transaction procedures, portfolio monitoring

### Graduate (FIN 5xx)
- **FIN 501** Programming in Finance -- Python for finance: data management, optimization, simulation, regression, portfolio, time-series
- **FIN 502** Fintech and Blockchain for Finance -- AI, deep learning, blockchain, cryptofinance, smart contracts, mobile payments
- **FIN 503** Fintech and Digital Innovation Fund -- Fintech case studies, student-managed fintech portfolio
