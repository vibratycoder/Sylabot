### Capstone project

A capstone project is a culminating academic experience where students apply the full spectrum of knowledge and skills acquired throughout their program to solve a real-world business problem. In the MSBA context, it typically involves working with an industry partner on an authentic analytics challenge from problem definition through solution delivery.

- **Project lifecycle:** Problem scoping and stakeholder alignment, data acquisition and assessment, exploratory analysis, methodology selection, model development, validation, insight generation, presentation of findings and recommendations
- **Deliverables:** Project proposal, interim progress reports, technical documentation, final presentation to stakeholders, written report with methodology and findings, potentially a deployed model or dashboard
- **Team dynamics:** Cross-functional collaboration, project management (agile/scrum methodologies), version control (Git), task delegation, conflict resolution, meeting facilitation
- **Evaluation criteria:** Technical rigor, business relevance and impact, quality of communication, creativity of approach, reproducibility, ethical considerations
- **Practical relevance:** The capstone bridges classroom learning and professional practice, providing portfolio-worthy work that demonstrates end-to-end analytics capabilities to potential employers. It develops the project management, stakeholder communication, and independent problem-solving skills that distinguish successful analytics professionals.

### Applied AI

Applied AI refers to the practical implementation of artificial intelligence techniques to solve specific, real-world business problems. Unlike theoretical AI research, applied AI focuses on delivering measurable business value through working solutions that integrate into existing business processes and systems.

- **Implementation spectrum:** From simple rule-based automation to advanced deep learning; selecting the appropriate level of AI complexity for each problem (often simpler models outperform complex ones)
- **Common applications:** Predictive analytics (forecasting, classification), natural language processing (document analysis, chatbots), computer vision (quality inspection, document OCR), recommendation systems, optimization engines
- **Development process:** Business problem definition, data assessment and preparation, baseline model establishment, iterative model improvement, A/B testing in production, monitoring and maintenance
- **Production considerations:** Model serving infrastructure, latency requirements, scalability, monitoring for data drift and model degradation, fallback mechanisms, explainability for stakeholders
- **Practical relevance:** Applied AI skills are in high demand as organizations seek professionals who can move beyond theoretical knowledge to deliver working AI solutions. Success requires balancing technical excellence with business acumen, understanding when AI adds value versus when simpler approaches suffice.

### Business analytics integration

Business analytics integration is the practice of combining multiple analytical methods, tools, and data sources into cohesive solutions that address complex business challenges holistically. It emphasizes connecting the full analytics value chain -- from data engineering through modeling to business impact.

- **Integration dimensions:** Combining descriptive (what happened), diagnostic (why), predictive (what will happen), and prescriptive (what should we do) analytics into end-to-end solutions
- **Technical integration:** Connecting data pipelines, ML models, visualization dashboards, and business applications; APIs and microservices architecture; real-time vs. batch processing decisions
- **Cross-functional collaboration:** Working with IT for infrastructure, business units for domain expertise, leadership for strategic alignment, and legal/compliance for governance
- **Measuring impact:** Defining KPIs before project start, establishing baselines, tracking business outcomes (not just model metrics), calculating ROI of analytics initiatives
- **Practical relevance:** The most impactful analytics professionals are those who can integrate multiple techniques and perspectives into solutions that drive business outcomes. This integrative capability -- combining technical skills with business understanding and communication -- is the hallmark of a mature analytics professional and the ultimate goal of the MSBA program.
