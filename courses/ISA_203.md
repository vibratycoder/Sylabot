### Business systems

Business systems are the various types of information systems that organizations use at different management levels to process transactions, support decisions, and drive strategic planning. These systems form a hierarchy, with each level serving distinct organizational needs from daily operations to executive oversight.

- **Transaction Processing Systems (TPS)**: Handle routine, day-to-day transactions such as payroll, order processing, and inventory updates at the operational level
- **Management Information Systems (MIS)**: Generate summary and exception reports from TPS data to help tactical managers monitor and control organizational performance
- **Decision Support Systems (DSS)**: Provide analytical tools for mid-level managers to evaluate complex scenarios, run simulations, and compare alternatives for semi-structured decisions
- **Executive Support Systems (ESS)**: Deliver high-level dashboards and strategic insights to senior leadership for long-term planning

**Practical relevance**: Understanding the hierarchy of business systems helps professionals select the right tools for each organizational level. TPS data feeds into MIS and DSS, which in turn inform executive decisions, creating an integrated flow of information that supports the entire organization.

### IT ethics

IT ethics encompasses the moral principles and professional standards that govern how technology is developed, deployed, and used within organizations and society. It addresses critical questions about privacy, fairness, accountability, and the societal impact of technological systems.

- **Privacy concerns**: Data collection practices, informed consent, protection of minors, electronic surveillance, and workplace monitoring
- **Data protection**: Safeguarding personal information from breaches, ensuring secure storage, and complying with regulations like GDPR and CCPA
- **AI and algorithmic ethics**: Addressing bias in machine learning models, facial recognition misuse, and ensuring fairness in automated decision-making
- **Professional responsibility**: Do no harm, improve quality of life, establish accountability practices, and maintain confidentiality

**Practical relevance**: As organizations collect and process ever-larger volumes of personal data, IT ethics has moved from an academic concern to a business imperative. Professionals must understand ethical frameworks to make responsible decisions about data usage, system design, and technology deployment that balance business goals with societal well-being.

### Security

Information security focuses on protecting data and systems from unauthorized access, modification, and destruction. The CIA Triad -- Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability -- provides the foundational framework for designing and evaluating security policies and controls across all types of organizations.

- **Confidentiality**: Protects information from unauthorized access through encryption, access controls, authentication, and secure communication channels
- **Integrity**: Ensures data remains accurate and unaltered, guarding against both malicious tampering and accidental corruption
- **Availability**: Maintains system uptime and data accessibility for authorized users, defended through redundancy, backups, and disaster recovery planning
- **Standards and compliance**: Organizations align with frameworks such as NIST SP 800-53, ISO 27001, and SOC 2 Type II to demonstrate security posture

**Practical relevance**: Cybersecurity threats -- including phishing, ransomware, insider threats, and social engineering -- are constant risks for every organization. A solid understanding of security fundamentals enables professionals to identify vulnerabilities, implement appropriate controls, and contribute to a security-aware organizational culture.

### Database application development

Database application development involves designing, building, and deploying software that interacts with a database to store, retrieve, and manipulate data. The CRUD model -- Create, Read, Update, Delete -- defines the four fundamental operations that form the backbone of virtually every database-driven application.

- **Create**: Adding new records to the database (SQL INSERT statement)
- **Read**: Retrieving existing data from the database (SQL SELECT statement)
- **Update**: Modifying existing records (SQL UPDATE statement)
- **Delete**: Removing records from the database (SQL DELETE statement)
- **Development process**: Includes requirements gathering, database schema design (tables, relationships, constraints), building the user interface, writing application logic, and testing
- **Key principles**: Data integrity through constraints, normalized table design to reduce redundancy, and secure access control

**Practical relevance**: Database applications power everything from customer portals to inventory systems. In this course, students build a functional database application for a business case, gaining hands-on experience with the full development cycle from schema design through deployment.
