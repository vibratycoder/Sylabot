# ISA 391: Information Systems and Analytics Internship

**Credits:** 3  
**Level:** undergraduate  
**Prerequisites:** None

## Course Description
Supervised employment applying ISA principles. Students work at least ten hours per week, meet periodically with a supervising faculty member.

## Key Topics
- Professional experience
- Applied ISA skills

### Professional experience

Professional experience in ISA refers to supervised employment where students apply information systems and analytics principles in real-world workplace settings. Internships provide students with applicable educational experiences that enhance academic qualifications and prepare them for post-graduation employment, with employers citing applicable work experience as the most crucial factor when evaluating job applicants.

**Key Concepts:**
- **Supervised workplace learning:** Interns work under the guidance of IT professionals to apply classroom knowledge to real-world projects, developing technical skills, problem-solving abilities, and professional competencies.
- **Career pathway development:** Entry-level positions often include junior systems analyst or IT support specialist roles, with advancement leading to senior systems analyst, IT project manager, or information systems manager positions.
- **Salary impact:** Students who complete internships can increase their starting salary by up to $5,000, with ISA graduates earning $40,000 to $80,000 upon graduation depending on skills and experience.
- **Skill areas:** Interns typically assist with system maintenance, software testing, data management, network administration, cybersecurity, and database operations.

**Practical Relevance:** Internship experience bridges the gap between academic learning and industry expectations, making students significantly more competitive in the job market and providing clarity on career specialization areas such as cybersecurity, data analytics, or enterprise resource planning (ERP).

### Applied ISA skills

Applied ISA skills encompass the practical competencies that information systems and analytics professionals use to solve business problems, manage technology, and derive insights from data. These skills span four functional categories: analytical thinking, data handling, technical execution, and communication.

**Key Concepts:**
- **Technical competencies:** Proficiency in SQL, Python, R, spreadsheets, and visualization tools for data collection, cleaning, transformation, and analysis.
- **Business acumen:** The ability to translate data insights into actionable business decisions, understanding what numbers mean for organizational strategy and outcomes.
- **Analytical thinking:** Problem framing, hypothesis testing, and logical reasoning applied to real-world business challenges.
- **Communication skills:** Presenting findings effectively and aligning data-driven insights with stakeholder needs across an organization.

**Practical Relevance:** Despite specialized education, research indicates a persistent gap between the knowledge students graduate with and the skills required by industry for entry-level data analyst roles. Applied ISA skill development through internships and hands-on projects helps close this gap by demonstrating how to practically employ technologies in a business setting to drive workplace efficiencies and solve real-world problems.
