### E-business

**Definition:** E-business (electronic business) is the conduct of all business activities, transactions, and processes over the internet and digital networks, encompassing not just buying and selling but also supply chain management, customer service, employee collaboration, and internal operations.

- **E-business vs. e-commerce:** E-commerce focuses specifically on buying and selling goods/services online, while e-business is the broader category covering all digitally conducted business operations
- **Key models** include B2B (business-to-business), B2C (business-to-consumer), C2C (consumer-to-consumer), and C2B (consumer-to-business)
- **Core components** include a digital storefront or platform, payment processing systems, supply chain integration, customer relationship management, and data analytics
- **Strategic elements** involve SEO/SEM for traffic acquisition, mobile-friendly user experience, analytics-driven decision making, and scalability planning
- **Revenue models** range from direct sales and subscriptions to advertising, commissions, and freemium offerings

**Practical relevance:** E-business has transformed how organizations operate across every industry. Understanding e-business models and strategies is essential for any business professional, as companies increasingly rely on digital channels for revenue generation, customer engagement, and operational efficiency.

### E-commerce models

**Definition:** E-commerce models classify online commercial transactions by the relationship between the parties involved. The four foundational types are B2B, B2C, C2C, and C2B, each with distinct characteristics, revenue structures, and market dynamics.

- **B2B (Business-to-Business):** Transactions between businesses such as manufacturers and wholesalers; characterized by high-volume orders, longer sales cycles, and contract-based relationships. The global B2B market is roughly 5x the size of B2C
- **B2C (Business-to-Consumer):** The most common model, where businesses sell directly to end consumers (e.g., Amazon, online retailers); features shorter sales cycles and impulse-driven purchasing
- **C2C (Consumer-to-Consumer):** Individuals sell to other individuals via third-party platforms (e.g., eBay, Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace); low barrier to entry for sellers
- **Marketplace model:** Platforms like Amazon and Alibaba act as intermediaries connecting multiple sellers with buyers, earning revenue through commissions and fees
- **Subscription and SaaS models** provide recurring revenue through regular payments for ongoing access to products or services

**Practical relevance:** Understanding e-commerce models helps entrepreneurs and business analysts select the right approach for their market. Each model has different requirements for logistics, payment processing, customer acquisition, and platform technology.

### Internet protocols

**Definition:** Internet protocols are standardized rules and conventions that govern how data is transmitted, routed, and received across computer networks. The TCP/IP protocol suite forms the foundation of all internet communication.

- **TCP (Transmission Control Protocol)** provides reliable, connection-oriented communication ensuring packets arrive in order and without errors
- **IP (Internet Protocol)** handles addressing and routing, using IP addresses to direct data packets to their correct destination
- **HTTP/HTTPS** governs how web pages and data are transferred between browsers and servers; HTTPS adds SSL/TLS encryption for security. HTTP accounts for over 90% of internet traffic
- **DNS (Domain Name System)** translates human-readable domain names (e.g., google.com) into numerical IP addresses, functioning as the internet's phone directory
- **The TCP/IP model** consists of four layers: Application, Transport, Internet, and Network Access, each handling distinct communication responsibilities

**Practical relevance:** Understanding internet protocols is essential for e-business because every online transaction, web page load, and API call relies on these protocols. Knowledge of HTTP/HTTPS is critical for web development and security, while DNS and TCP/IP understanding supports network troubleshooting and infrastructure planning.

### Digital marketing

**Definition:** Digital marketing encompasses all marketing efforts that use digital channels and technologies to reach, engage, and convert customers. It includes strategies across search engines, social media, email, content platforms, and mobile applications.

- **SEO (Search Engine Optimization)** improves organic search visibility by optimizing website content, meta tags, site structure, and technical performance for higher search engine rankings
- **SEM (Search Engine Marketing)** uses paid search advertising (e.g., Google Ads) with keyword bidding to drive immediate traffic through sponsored search results
- **Content marketing** builds long-term credibility through valuable content such as blog posts, videos, infographics, ebooks, and podcasts
- **Social media marketing (SMM)** promotes products and services through organic posts and paid ads on platforms like Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, and Facebook, offering precise audience targeting
- **Email marketing** delivers personalized messages directly to subscribers for nurturing leads and retaining customers

**Practical relevance:** High-performing brands combine multiple digital marketing channels rather than relying on a single approach. Digital marketing skills are among the most in-demand in business today, as organizations across all sectors shift budgets from traditional to digital channels for measurable ROI and data-driven optimization.

### Online advertising

**Definition:** Online advertising refers to the practice of delivering promotional messages to consumers through internet-based channels. Major models include pay-per-click (PPC), display advertising, and programmatic advertising, each with distinct pricing and targeting mechanisms.

- **PPC (Pay-Per-Click)** charges advertisers only when a user clicks their ad; primarily used on search engines (Google Ads) and social media platforms for targeted, intent-driven traffic
- **Display advertising** uses visual formats (banners, images, videos) placed on websites and apps, commonly priced on a CPM (cost-per-thousand-impressions) basis for brand awareness campaigns
- **Programmatic advertising** automates the buying and selling of ad inventory through algorithms and real-time bidding (RTB), enabling precise audience targeting at massive scale across billions of ad placements
- **Key metrics** include click-through rate (CTR), conversion rate, cost-per-acquisition (CPA), and return on ad spend (ROAS)
- **Retargeting/remarketing** shows ads to users who previously visited a website, improving conversion rates through repeated exposure

**Practical relevance:** Online advertising is the primary revenue model for platforms like Google and Meta, and a critical customer acquisition channel for businesses of all sizes. Understanding these models helps marketers allocate budgets effectively and measure campaign performance with precision.

### Digital delivery

**Definition:** Digital delivery refers to the distribution of products, services, and content to end users entirely through electronic channels, eliminating the need for physical media or in-person transactions. This includes streaming media, SaaS platforms, digital downloads, and cloud-based services.

- **SaaS (Software as a Service)** delivers applications over the internet on a subscription basis, with vendors handling maintenance, upgrades, security, and hosting (e.g., Salesforce, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365)
- **Streaming services** deliver media content (music, video, games) in real time without requiring permanent downloads (e.g., Netflix, Spotify, YouTube)
- **Digital downloads** provide one-time transfers of software, ebooks, music, or games through platforms like Steam, Apple App Store, or Google Play
- **Distribution channels** include self-serve subscriptions, app store marketplaces (Salesforce AppExchange, Google Workspace Marketplace), API partnerships, and white-label reselling
- **Content delivery networks (CDNs)** optimize global distribution speed and reliability by caching content on geographically distributed servers

**Practical relevance:** Digital delivery has become the dominant distribution model across software, media, and education industries. SaaS alone is now the primary way companies deliver applications. Understanding digital delivery models is essential for product strategy, pricing decisions, and building scalable distribution infrastructure.
